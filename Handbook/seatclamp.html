Teamwork Diverging and Testing

For Praxis 1, me and my team were coming up with solutions on how to improve the posture of people who work while commuting. Initially we started diverging and come up with a few concepts. One of the earliest concepts that actually got modified and used as part of our final design choice, which is shown in Figure 1. This was meant to be a clamp that attaches to the bottom of your seat and has a hinge that allows it to rotate. It also has a table at the top that allows it to hold the laptop. I came up with the initial idea for this design while brainstorming with my team through lotus blossom. The measurement matrix that we used to compare designs is shown in Figure 2.

Testing the Prototype

Three main tests were performed on the prototype. The first was a stability test where the laptop was placed onto the prototype. Since the prototype was made of the same material of the final design we could raise the prototype until the laptop started to slide and then used the angle at which it slided to determine the coefficient of friction between the design and the laptop, which is shown in Figure 5. The second test that was performed testing the flexibility of the hinge and the users ability to manipulate the prototype, which is shown in the video in Figure 6. The final test was testing how much the posture of the user imporved when using the prototype, as shown in Figure 7.
