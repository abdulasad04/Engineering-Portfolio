Initial Construction Process

For my first year introductory Physics course, I had to build a pendulum and experiment with the period and how it was affected by the length of the string attached to the pendulum. To perform this experiment I needed a pendulum that could change length and would reduce unvertainty as much as possible. With these as my basic requirements I went on to brainstorming different designs, shown on the left (insert picture). The construction process for this prototype is shown in Figures 2 and 3.

Refining the Design

After the initial design I realized that I had to go back and refine the frame to address to major issues:

  1. The lack of adjustability of the string
  2. The eliptical motion of the pendulum as I let it go

Firstly, the length of the string was very hard to adjust as it was taped to the top of the pendulum and removing the tape was tedious and would also require me to change the tape once it lost its stickiness. So I decided to reiterate the design with a mechanism on the top of the frame of the pendulum which is shown in the video. This made ajustments much faster. I also added a ramps with a dashed line along the ramp to help me align where the pendulum was let go. This took away a degree of freedom that the pendulum had when I was simply letting go of it with my hand, which reduced uncertainty. The ramp is shown in the Figure 4.
