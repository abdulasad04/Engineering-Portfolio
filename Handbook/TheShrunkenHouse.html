Initial Planning Process

For my design course, praxis 2, me and my team were tasked with designing a solution to help blind curlers play without help from their coaches or teammates. Initially we had to rescope as the design space felt too broad. We decided to focus on mapping the house where all the curling rocks are. First we started brainstorming ideas and tried to draw down as many as we could think of as a team, which is shown in Figure 1. One of our solutions was a miniature house with solenoids that would go up and down depending on whether or not there was a solenoid there. This allowed the stakeholders to feel out the orientation of the rocks in the house. Before starting to prototype this design, we found a reference design that controlled solenoids using an arduino, the circuit diagram of this is shown in Figure 2.

Prototyping the Design

Once we came up with the idea of the shrunken house, we began prototyping. Initially, we started on a small scale and tried to find a way to get just one solenoid to go up and down. This was more difficult than expected as an arduino can only supply 5V of electricity, but the solenoid needed 9V. Because of this, we had to use a power supply to power the solenoid and a transistor to control the solenoid. A video of the initial workings of the solenoid is shown in Figure 3. After that, we began building our first prototype of the shrunken house using cardboard to hold the solenoids in place. A video of our first prototype is shown in Figure 4. We then went on to create an engineering drawing of The Shrunken House to help further convey the scalability and future prospects of the design, which is shown in Figure 5.
